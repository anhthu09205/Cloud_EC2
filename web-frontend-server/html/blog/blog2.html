Lithography
Machines

Invisible light, atomic precision — the machines that quietly power the modern digital world.

Lithography machines are the backbone of semiconductor manufacturing. They project complex circuit patterns onto silicon wafers, enabling the creation of billions of transistors on a single chip.

Among all technologies, EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet) lithography represents the peak of engineering precision — allowing chips to reach nanometer scale.

Modern civilization runs on silicon — and silicon is written by light.

Core Technologies

Precision Optics

Each machine costs over $150 million and consists of thousands of ultra-precise components working in perfect synchronization.

Without ASML's lithography technology, modern CPUs, GPUs, AI systems, and smartphones would not exist.
